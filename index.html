Maybe it used to sound offensive, but times have changed. Today, a nerd is a person who is very passionate about their interests, such as science, computers, games, or comics. Sometimes a little withdrawn, but very intelligent and erudite in their subject.

History of the word

The word "nerd" first appeared in the U.S. in the 1950s and was used as an insult for kids seen as boring or overly focused on school. By the 1960s, TV and movies started showing nerds as shy, quirky, but very smart characters. From the 1980s and especially the 1990s, the term became more positive: a "nerd" is now someone deeply passionate about science, technology, programming, or other intellectual hobbies — and often proud of it.
